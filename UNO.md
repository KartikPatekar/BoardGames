Sure! **Uno** is a popular card game with simple rules. Here are the **official rules for Uno**:

---

## **Objective**
Be the first player to get rid of all your cards.

---

## **Setup**
- **Players:** 2-10
- **Deck:** 108 cards (red, yellow, green, blue; numbers 0-9; action cards)

---

## **Gameplay**
**1. Starting**
- Each player gets **7 cards**.
- The rest are placed as a draw pile. Flip one card to start the discard pile.

**2. Player Turn**
- Match a card from your hand to the top card on the discard pile by **color, number, or symbol**.
- If you can't play, **draw one card** from the draw pile.
  - If you can play the drawn card, you may do so immediately.
- If not, play passes to the next player.

---

## **Special Cards**
| Card            | Effect                             |
|-----------------|------------------------------------|
| **Skip**        | Next player is skipped             |
| **Reverse**     | Reverses turn order                |
| **Draw Two**    | Next player draws 2 cards & loses a turn |
| **Wild**        | Playable any time; choose color    |
| **Wild Draw Four** | Playable if you have no matching color; choose color; next player draws 4 cards & loses a turn |

---

## **Uno!**
- When you have **one card left**, you must shout "Uno!" before your turn ends. If caught not saying it, draw 2 cards.

---

## **Winning**
- When you play your last card, **you win the round**.
- Points are usually scored based on the cards left in opponents' hands. First to **500 points wins the game** (optional).

---

## **Other Notes**
- If the draw pile runs out, shuffle the discard pile to refill.
- You can only play Wild Draw Four if you have no card matching the color in play (challengeable by the next player).
